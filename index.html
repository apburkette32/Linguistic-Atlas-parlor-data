Linguistic Atlas Project

responses to 'parlor' question

Map authored by apburkette32

Data for this map comes from two of the Linguistic Atlas Project regional surveys: the Linguistic Atlas of New England (LANE) and the Linguistic Atlas of the Middle and South Atlantic States (LAMSAS). Speakers in these regions were asked what they called "the place in the house where you have chairs for people to talk who come to call". The most common responses were 'parlor,' 'living room,' and 'sitting room.'
